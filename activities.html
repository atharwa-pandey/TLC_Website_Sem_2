The Teaching-Learning Workshop for Next Generation Academicians (November 2021)

“The Teaching-Learning Workshop for Next Generation Academicians” was organized by the Teaching Learning Centre (TLC), BITS Pilani - Pilani Campus on November 27, 2021 for the research scholars, recently admitted to the PhD programme of the institute. Coordinated by Dr. Gautam Singhvi, Dr. Meetha Shenoy and Prof. Prashant Uday Manohar, the workshop aimed to provided mentorship for these future academicians in meticulously conducting their teaching activities with great satisfaction. About 148 research scholars actively participated in the workshop. The day-long workshop started at 9:30 am when Prof. Sudhirkumar Barai, Director, Pilani Campus along with Prof. Shibani Khanra Jha, Faculty-Incharge, TLC and the coordinators lighted the lamp. Prof. Jha briefed the participants about the relevance of the workshop and also provided the summary of events conducted by TLC in the previous year. Prof. Barai addressed the gathering and guided with his words of wisdom. This was followed by a group photograph of the participants and the resource persons.
The technical session started with the talk by Prof. Surekha Bhanot, one of the seniormost faculty of the institute, on “Expanding your skills, experience and expertise as Research Scholar”. TLC was honoured to have Prof. Chandra Shekhar, Sernior Professor Emeritus of the institute and Chairperson and Chancellor of the Academy of Science and Innovatuve Research (AcSIR, India) as a resource person for the workshop. He delivered a talk “Life after PhD” and was highly appreciated.
The workshop also witnessed the enlightening talks, namely, “Inculcating scientific temperament in young minds for laboratory practices” by Prof. Anshuman Dalvi, “Communication impact on content delivery” by Prof. Pushp Lata and the talks by Prof. Tapomoy Guha Sarkar and Prof. Aniruddha Roy on “Ethical Practices” and by Prof. P. Srinivasan, Dr. G. Muthukumar and Dr. Pawan Ajmera on “Laboratory Instruction with an Emphasis on Assessment”.
Certificates of participation were distributed to the participants in the concluding session. The workshop ended with the vote of thanks presented by Dr. Singhvi.

ICON BITS Conference (July 2021)

The three-day Inaugural International Conference on Best Innovative Teaching Strategies (ICON-BITS) 2021 was organized by the Teaching Learning Center, BITS Pilani (Pilani Campus) from 29 Jul 2021 to 31 Jul 2021. This event received an overwhelming response from academia- both in India and abroad. 98 research papers authored by more than 225 academicians were selected for presentation in this conference. Abstracts of all these articles have been published during the conference and is available publicly. More than 75 of these articles have been selected for publication in the conference proceedings published by Macmillan. The proceedings will also be indexed by SSRN- a unit of Elsevier. These papers will be perpetually available publicly on the SSRN platform.
The conference participants were enlightened by talks delivered by invited speakers from many reputed academic institutions of the world, like Carnegie Mellon University, University of Houston, MIT, University of North Texas, University of Oklahoma, Texas A&M University, besides eminent people from the industry, including Microsoft and eVidyaloka. The contribution of the advisory board which included eminent academicians from New York University, Purdue University, Texas A&M University, Carnegie Mellon University, University of Wisconsin, University of Houston, IIT Kharagpur, National Institute of Technical Teachers' Training and Research, NIT Durgapur and our own BITS Pilani ensured that the conference was truly international in nature.

ITW I and II (March 2021): The Intensive Teaching Workshop (ITW-2021)

The Intensive Teaching Workshop (ITW-2021) was organized by the Teaching Learning Centre on 21st and 27th of March, 2021. Coordinated by Dr. Prashant U. Manohar, Dr. Meetha V. Shenoy and Dr. Gautam Singhvi, the workshop witnessed participation of 43 new faculty members of the BITS community and 34 resource persons including 9 TLC team members.
The ITW Part-I was conducted on 21st March from 8:00 am to 6:15 pm. The Faculty-In-charge, TLC, Prof. Pushp Lata oriented the participants to ITW-2021 during the inaugural session. Honourable Vice Chancellor, Prof. Souvik Bhattacharya and Honourable Director, Prof. Sudhirkumar Barai graced the occasion by addressing the gathering in the inaugural session. The technical sessions conducted by 15 resource persons covered variety of interactive talks and practice sessions based on the modules, namely, ‘Teaching Profession – Personal Perspectives’, ‘Domains of Learning and Instructional Objectives’, ‘Academic Regulations of BITS Pilani and its implementation in Teaching, Assessment and Grading’, ‘Flipped mode of Lecture Delivery: Opportunities and Challenges’, ‘Technology-Enabled Teaching and Learning’, ‘Assessment and Evaluation’ and ‘Better Student Engagement for Enhancing Teaching-Learning Effectiveness’.
ITW Part-II was conducted on March 27, 2021, from 9:00 am to 1:00 pm. The participants were randomly distributed into six groups. Each group had four resource persons including one TLC member. Each participant demonstrated his/her teaching skills by presenting a short lecture of 20 minutes on the topic of their own choice, with balanced use of blackboard-work, slides, etc. The resource persons noted the observations and shared the same at the end of each presentation.
Feedback was collected from participants at the end of Part-I as well as Part-II and participation certificates were distributed at the end of Part-II.

SAC and TLC jointly Organized Survey on the proceeding of the Online Semester (November 2020))

Both the Students' Academic Cell (SAC) and the Teaching Learning Centre (TLC) realized that due to the COVID-19 pandemic, all the teachers and students were forced to redefine the teaching-learning process in many ways. A remarkable effort was put by both the teaching fraternity and BITS students to mimic the "regular" teaching-learning process in the turbulent times. Earlier, the feedback was taken on the academic challenges that were faced by both the stake holders due to this emergency shift to online teaching. Based on the survey results, an online teaching workshop was organized by TLC BITS before this pre-planned online I semester 2020- 21. As uncertainty regarding the reopening of the campus prevailed, the Students' Academic Cell and the Teaching Learning Centre jointly solicited feedback using Google forms on the online mode of education, both from faculty and students in the month of November 2020. This Survey aimed at gauging the effectiveness of teaching and learning using the new methods of teaching and communication like Slack, Mentimeter, Nalanda discussion forums, etc., from the point of view of both the stakeholders. It also focused on understanding the students' opinions regarding what changes have worked and what aspects need revisiting for better students' engagement in learning. The Survey also intended to know the teachers' challenges in adapting the online pedagogical practices and their suggestions to enhance the academic rigor and effectiveness.
A staggering number of 1456 students and 110 faculty members participated in this online Survey enthusiastically. Keeping in view the student and the faculty community was extremely busy due to the upcoming comprehensive exams, both SAC and TLC jointly decided to share the results in an open event in the next semester.

SEER (Sharing Experience and Educational Research)

The SEER (Sharing Experience and Educational Research) series is envisioned during the Fall of 2018 to be a once in a month TLC forum activity to discuss research articles in teaching-learning and to share unique pedagogical practices by the BITS faculty members. It was planned that a SEER session would typically have two sessions of thirty minutes each where a faculty member would present his/her research on pedagogy and its best practices in the first half, while another faculty member would share his/her teaching experience with the BITS community. The aim is to generate discussion and debate and to learn from each other.
The following SEER talks were organized in the year 2019:
• The first session of SEER was held on 31/01/2019 where Dr. Pankaj Kumar Sharma from the Department of Biological Sciences discussed a paper titled “Can Undergraduate (Biology) Students Learn to Ask Higher Level Questions?” by G. Marback-Ad and P.G. Sokolove. Prof. Ashoke Sarkar, our Director for Pilani campus, was the second SEER speaker who shared his valuable experience regarding teaching in BITS Pilani in the second half of the session.
• The second session of SEER took place on 28/02/2019 where Dr. Kaushar Vaidya from the Department of Physics discussed a paper titled “Peer Instruction: Ten years of experience and results” by Catherine Crouch and Eric Mazur. Prof. P. Srinivasan from the Department of Mechanical Engineering shared his insightful experience of classroom teaching in BITS Pilani.
• The third session of SEER was held on 26/08/2019 where Prof. Tapomoy Guha Sarkar discussed the original work by Benjamin Bloom on the taxonomy of educational objectives and demonstrated how the taxonomy can be used to set balanced question papers and devise a metric for teaching-learning outcomes in a talk titled “Bloomi-ing Question Paper”.
• The fourth session of SEER, on 24/09/2019 saw Prof. Navneet Goyal delivered a talk titled “Relating Machine Learning concepts to Academic/Pedagogical Practices” where he explained how he had simplified machine learning concepts in classroom by drawing analogies from pedagogical practices and academic models
